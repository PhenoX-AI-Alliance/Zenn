## 2. Implementing DDD for Environmental Data

Environmental data is inherently complex, characterized by spatial-temporal dimensions, high-frequency sensor streams, and multi-layered metadata. Applying DDD ensures that our software remains resilient to the evolving nature of Earth science.

### The Aggregate Root: `EnvironmentalObservation`
In our DDD model, an `EnvironmentalObservation` acts as the Aggregate Root. It encapsulates:
*   **Sensor Identity:** The source of the telemetry.
*   **Spatial Coordinates:** Geo-referenced data points.
*   **Temporal Context:** ISO-8601 timestamps with precision metadata.
