## 1. DDD-Based System Design for Environmental Intelligence

To model the Earth’s complex ecosystems, we must move beyond monolithic data lakes. Using **Domain-Driven Design (DDD)**, we decompose the environmental domain into manageable, high-fidelity **Bounded Contexts**:

*   **The Sensor Telemetry Context:** Responsible for ingesting high-frequency data from satellite and IoT ground stations. This context prioritizes event-driven architecture and immutable event logs.
*   **The Climate Modeling Context:** A core domain that encapsulates the business logic for predictive analytics. By isolating this logic, we ensure that changes in climate algorithms do not ripple into our ingestion or reporting layers.
*   **The Optimization Context:** An adaptive layer that translates model outputs into actionable strategies for resource management (e.g., carbon capture optimization or water distribution).

By enforcing strict **Context Maps**, we ensure that our system remains decoupled, allowing teams to iterate on specific environmental models without risking the stability of the entire infrastructure.

## 2. Accelerating Velocity with Claude Code

The complexity of environmental data requires a development velocity that traditional manual coding cannot sustain. In 2026, **Claude Code** serves as the primary engine for our development lifecycle.

### AI-Driven Workflow Integration:
*   **Context-Aware Refactoring:** By feeding our DDD boundary definitions into Claude Code, we ensure that new feature implementations strictly adhere to our architectural constraints, preventing "architectural drift."
*   **Automated Formal Verification:** Claude Code assists in writing TLA+ specifications for our distributed systems, ensuring that our environmental data pipelines remain resilient during network partitions or sensor failures.
*   **Rapid Prototyping of Heuristics:** We use Claude to iterate on the optimization algorithms within our Bounded Contexts, allowing developers to focus on high-level system strategy while the AI handles boilerplate implementation and unit test coverage.

This synergy between human architectural oversight and AI execution allows us to deploy updates to environmental monitoring models in hours rather than weeks.
